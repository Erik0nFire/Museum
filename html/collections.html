Maritime Bronze Weight

Material: Cast bronze alloy (copper and tin)

Size/Weight/Shape: 6 cm diameter; 210 g; circular disk with engraved rim

Estimated Age: ~3200 years old (Late Bronze Age, ca. 1200 BCE)

Preservation State: Slight corrosion, form intact

Location Found: Ugarit port ruins, northern Levant coast (modern Syria)

Likely Purpose: Standardized trade weight for maritime commerce

Evidence of Use: Polishing on edges; faint impressions from scale arms

Manufacture Clues: Mold casting seams visible; decorative linear pattern around edge

Found With: Amphorae fragments, bronze ingots, merchant’s seal

Burial or Habitat Context: Storage chamber near collapsed warehouse

Symbolism: Possibly inscribed with the symbol for “balance” — fairness in trade

Comparison: Similar weights found in Mycenaean and Cypriot coastal trade sites
